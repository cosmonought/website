About Neta DAO

A nonprofit institution built on civic principle.

We believe that public blockchains — open, permissionless, and governed by their communities — deserve permanent public institutions to serve them. Neta DAO exists to be one.

Mission

Crypto civics for the Interchain

Neta DAO is a nonprofit organization serving the Cosmos ecosystem and the Interchain at large. Our mission is crypto civics: the idea that participation in blockchain governance is a form of public life, and that it deserves the same seriousness, institutional support, and open access as civic participation in any democracy.

We pursue this through three interlocking programs: validator services that directly support the security and decentralization of Interchain networks; governance participation through our on-chain DAO; and education through Neta DAO Academy, which offers free public seminars and weekly open meetings.

We are not a protocol, a fund, or a product. We are an institution — one that happens to run on-chain.

Origin

Born from the first proposals of the Juno Network

The $NETA token was not created by this DAO. It was airdropped by the Juno Core-1 team to wallets that had participated in the earliest governance proposals of the Juno Network — a deliberate recognition of civic engagement, not just economic activity.

Those early participants had done something unusual: they showed up to vote before it was profitable to do so. The airdrop was a statement that this kind of participation had value beyond price speculation.

Neta DAO formed around these holders and their shared history. We are stewards of a community defined by its commitment to governance. Everything we do extends that founding act.

Our governance is defined by the Neta DAO Constitution, ratified on-chain through a governance proposal. The Constitution establishes the structure of the DAO: a main governance token DAO, a Treasury subDAO, an Operations subDAO, and an Executor subDAO — each with defined roles and on-chain accountability.

Weekly Congressional meetings are open to all members and serve as the primary forum for DAO business — discussing proposals, reviewing operations, and deliberating on the direction of the organization. These are not calls. They are the legislative sessions of a living institution.

Our validator was active on Juno and other Interchain chains. Though validator operations are currently paused, restoring and expanding them remains a core organizational goal.

Infrastructure

Validator Services

Neta DAO has operated a validator on the Juno Network, participating directly in block production and network security. Validator commissions have supported the DAO treasury.

Validator operations are currently paused. Restoring them — and extending to additional Interchain chains — is an active organizational priority subject to governance.
