Discussion Series

Coining Reason

The ideas of economists and political philosophers, both when they are right and when they are wrong, are more powerful than is commonly understood. Indeed the world is ruled by little else. Practical men, who believe themselves to be quite exempt from any intellectual influences, are usually the slaves of some defunct economist…. I am sure that the power of vested interests is vastly exaggerated compared with the gradual encroachment of ideas. — John Maynard Keynes

Coining Reason explores ideas related to Web3 through texts spanning economics, philosophy, politics, psychoanalysis, law, ecology, business, art, and religion. The breadth reflects an urgent necessity: financialization means practically all aspects of life are now subsumed by the rule and roil of coin, so that any single disciplinary approach is apt to miss those "constellations of facts" that index more potent realities.

Sex, and/or Love

Psychoanalysis and the Science of Desire

This seminar analyzes sexual desire through its principal science: psychoanalysis. Because desire is a force rather than an object, this "science of desire" questions the limits of ordinary empiricism by placing questioning — with its attendant themes of trust and doubt, obsession and uncertainty — at the center of existence. Thus, we will reinscribe the reality of reality through the unconscious. Topics include paid sex, consensual non-consent, the biology and evolution of sex, female orgasm, self-harm and self-pleasure, the ungovernable, and the futurity of romance.

Full Curriculum

Original Roadmap

The full Coining Reason curriculum spans ten units. Participants ultimately help determine the pace, scale, and scope of the investigation.

Unit I
The Internet
Web 1, 2, 3 — cyberspace, digital bodies, the blockchain application stack. Completed.
Unit II
Economics
Theories of money, surplus value, Bitcoin, banking, and the political economy of cryptocurrency. On hiatus.
Unit III
Politics
Sovereignty, constituent power, democracy, decentralization, labor after Fordism, and software politics.
Unit IV
Philosophy
Human nature, technology, individuation, crypto-anarchism, transcendental blockchain, and general economy.
Unit V
Law
Piracy, contract and tort law, blockchain as law, crypto and state, and securities regulation.
Unit VI
Ecology
Network and ecosystem, surplus enjoyment, surplus energy, and degrowth.
Unit VII
Business
The company, corporations, start-ups, organizational design, nonprofits, and DAOs.
Unit VIII
Art
NFTs, fiction and capital, collecting, performativity, and blockchain as art.
Unit IX
Religion
Ancient economy, sacred money, icon and economy, divine economy, and sacralization.
Unit X
Miscellany
Overflow texts from across the roadmap, available for supplementary discussion in any unit.

Full reading lists for all units: github.com/NetaDAOAcademy/CoiningReason ↗

Forthcoming

Fork: The Journal of Interchain Theory & Politics

Fork is a forthcoming digital journal dedicated to the theory and politics of decentralized systems. It will publish peer-reviewed work on Interchain governance, crypto civics, the philosophy of decentralization, and the political economy of Web3.

The journal takes its name from the blockchain concept of a fork — the moment when a shared history diverges and two possible futures emerge. We believe this is also a description of the political moment we are in.

Fork will be open access, community-governed, and eventually hosted at fork.netadao.org.
